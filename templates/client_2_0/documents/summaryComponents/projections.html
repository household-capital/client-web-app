    Explanatory notes

    Home Value

    Your home value is updated each year using the specified increase/decrease rate. The calculations assume a constant rate of interest (although in reality this rate will rise and fall).

    Interest Rate

    Interest rates are assumed to remain constant over the life of the loan. This loan has a variable rate of interest and should be expected to fluctuate over time. Interest is calculated daily and accrued monthly.

    Loan balance

    The outstanding loan balance is calculated each month by adding any planned loan drawdowns, interest charges and any fees to the outstanding loan balance from the previous month. These projections assume that no repayments are made during the life of the loan.

    Home Equity

    Your home equity is calculated by subtracting the outstanding loan from the value of your home. This is the value you might expect to receive if you sold your home, after repaying the loan. The home equity percentage is calculated by dividing your Home Equity by your Home Value.
